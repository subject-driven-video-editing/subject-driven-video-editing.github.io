SDVE: Subject-Driven Video Editing

Anonymous Submission

Abstract

Advanced text-guided generation models have facilitated the creation of high-quality and diverse images/videos based on textual descriptions. However, these models still face limitations in faithfully replicating the visual appearance of a specified reference image or object and generating innovative variations. These limitations can be more severe in the video-level generation because existing methods struggle with temporal consistency and precise control over subject appearance and integration. In this paper, we present a novel SDVE framework that delivers superior temporal coherence and enables user control, which contributes to personalized videos with customized subjects and satisfactory content re-creation. To ensure subject-driven edits and a visually coherent result, SDVE addresses key challenges by (1) providing reference images to guide precise edits, (2) employing a S.T.W (spatial-temporal window) attention mechanism to optimize computational efficiency, and (3) using mask-based latent editing to isolate subject modifications from their background, preserving the integrity of the original scene. Finally, SDVE facilitates targeted video manipulation through keyframe mask editing in the latent space and extends these modifications to non-keyframes via correspondence propagation. This ensures seamless subject edits, visually coherent results, and smooth, natural-looking subject interactions within the video. Extensive experiments demonstrate that SDVE outperforms existing methods in creating controlled and temporally consistent video editing.

Pipeline

Pipeline: SDVE enables subject-driven video editing through user-provided reference images. Utilizing both a chat agent-generated text description and the reference image, SDVE precisely edits the target subject throughout the video while maintaining overall visual coherence.

Method

Method: Our novel subject-driven video editing framework incorporates: 1) Reference image-based LoRA fine-tuning, 2) Spatial-temporal window attention for optimized keyframe generation, and 3) Mask-based latent blending for controlled edits and inter-frame propagation.
