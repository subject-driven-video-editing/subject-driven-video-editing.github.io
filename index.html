Abstract

Subject-driven video generation is pivotal for crafting videos that contain personalized subjects, ensuring a surge of interest in its potential and application from the research community. While current methodologies have made headway in generating such videos, there is a discernible gap in maintaining temporal consistency and precise control over the subject's background, positioning, and overall integration within a video sequence. This paper aims to bridge this gap by introducing an innovative approach to subject-driven video editing that focuses on the subject and ensures temporal coherence in the edited videos. We introduce a framework SDVE, encompassing the novel task: Subject-driven video editing. The task is inherently challenging as it demands not only the seamless insertion or replacement of subjects with customization in shape, texture, and color but also necessitates maintaining the contextual and temporal coherence of the entire video sequence. Our framework uses a subject-driven approach and deformation field propagation for video editing. It corrects shape distortions and fills missing areas, ensuring edits match the video's dynamics and subject prompts. Deformation fields apply edits uniformly across frames, keeping the subject's movement and interaction smooth. Extensive experiments show our method excels in creating controlled, temporally consistent video editing.

Pipeline

Left, Pipeline: we store all the attention maps in the DDIM inversion pipeline. At the editing stage of the DDIM denoising, we then fuse the attention maps with the stored attention maps using the proposed Attention Blending Block.

Right, Attention Blending Block: First, we replace the cross-attention maps of un-edited words~(e.g., road and countryside) with their maps using the source prompt during inversion. As for the edited words (e.g., posche car), we blend the self-attention maps during inversion and editing with an adaptive spatial mask obtained from cross-attention, which represents the areas that the user wants to edit.
